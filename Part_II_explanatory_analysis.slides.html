Part II - Explanatory Data Visualizations for Prosper Loan Data¶

by Kayode Fredrick ATOYEBI¶

Investigation Overview¶

Loans for Debt Consolidation, Business and Baby & Adoption gets the approval for higher amounts. Out of these three categories, Debt Consolidation is the money maker as it's the most popular and is projected to bring in more returns than other categories. Health workers, salespeople and computer programmers are the top people who love consolidating their debt. While healthworkers fetches the most returns, computer programmers are less likey to default. The analysis also reveal that approval for 3-year loans for this category should be dialled down. Focus should instead be on the 1-year loan as there is lesser chance of default

Dataset Overview¶

This Loan Data from Prosper (a peer-to-peer lending platform) contains 113,937 loan entries with 81 variables covering a range of loan terms, borrower demographics, and credit profiles. Loan entries listed before July 2009 were dropped and at the end of the wrangling processes, there were 76168 entries with 19 variables to explore

Debt Consolidation Loans¶

Debt consolidation loans account for about 70% of projected returns and 64% of all loan entries. From the chart, it appears that folks want to put their house in order and consolidate all their debt in anticipation of a new year as there's a surge from October through January

Occupation Comparison¶

While healthworkers, salespeople and computer programmers are estimated to bring the largest returns to the firm with debt consolidation loans, computer programmers are less likely to default or miss a payment followed by salespeople (who are in the sweet middle) and then healthworkers.

Best Loan Term¶

It appears that 3-year loans account for majority of debt consolidation loans defaulted or not paid back completely while 1-year loans are the least likely to be defaulted. The 1-year Debt Consolidation loans should be marketed more to folks particularly healthworkers, salespeople and computer programmers from the end of September each year.
